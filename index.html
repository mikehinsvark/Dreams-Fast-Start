R&D Tax Credits
IRS Section 41 — The Big Money Tool
What Qualifies for R&D Credits? Any business that invests in developing or improving products, processes, software, or formulas may qualify. The four-part test: (1) Technological in nature, (2) Permitted purpose, (3) Elimination of uncertainty, (4) Process of experimentation. Most businesses doing ANY innovation qualify without knowing it.
🏭 Industries That Qualify
  • Manufacturing — any process improvement or new product development
  • Software development — custom apps, platforms, algorithms
  • Food & beverage — new recipes, processes, packaging
  • Engineering & architecture — design and testing activities
  • Medical devices & pharmaceuticals — research and testing
  • Agriculture — new crop varieties, farming techniques
  • Construction — innovative building methods and materials
💰 Credit Calculation Basics
  • Federal credit = 20% of qualifying R&D expenses above a base amount
  • Alternative Simplified Credit (ASC): 14% of expenses above 50% of 3-yr avg
  • Qualifying expenses: wages, supplies, contract research
  • Startups: can offset up to $500K/yr in payroll taxes (PATH Act)
  • Can be claimed retroactively for 3 open tax years
  • Many states offer additional R&D credits on top of federal

FICA Tip Credits
IRS Section 45B — Dollar-for-Dollar Federal Credit
What Is the FICA Tip Credit? Employers pay FICA taxes (7.65%) on employee tips. Section 45B allows employers to claim a dollar-for-dollar federal tax credit for FICA taxes paid on tips above the federal minimum wage of $2.13/hour. It is refundable — meaning businesses get it back even if they owe no tax.
🍽️ Primary Target Industries
  • Restaurants and bars — highest density of tipped employees
  • Hotels and resorts — housekeeping, bellhops, valets
  • Casinos — dealers, cocktail servers, floor staff
  • Golf clubs and country clubs — caddies, servers, staff
  • Hair salons and spas — stylists and service providers
  • Food delivery and catering companies
💰 Credit Math Example
  • Restaurant with 20 tipped employees averaging $15/hr in tips
  • FICA on tips above $2.13/hr = 7.65% × ($15 - $2.13) × hours worked
  • 20 employees × 2,000 hrs × $12.87 × 7.65% = ~$39,300/year credit
  • Dollar-for-dollar reduction in federal taxes owed
  • Refundable: if taxes owed = $0, they still receive the full credit
  • Can be claimed retroactively for 3 open tax years

  • 5% on ALL production from your direct referrals
  • Must be qualified with 25 prospects in your tracker
  • Paid on every sale your referred reps make
  • Spreads the gap between your rank and your downline’s rank
  • Example: Exec MD (50%) + Consultant (25%) = 25% spread to you
  • Example: Senior AP (65%) + Consultant (20%) = 45% spread to you
📊 Spread Income Explained
  • Spread = difference between YOUR rank % and your downline’s %
  • You earn the spread on everything your downline produces
  • As your team grows in rank, your spread narrows — incentive to promote
  • This is passive team income — paid on production, not just sales
  • Combined with Generational Overrides = compounding empire income
  • Agency Partner + large team = maximum leverage on the comp plan
